WELCOME TO KAMPUNG WAI

Perlis' Most Beautiful Hidden River

Kampung Wai is a serene riverside village near Kuala Perlis, approximately 10 km from Kangar town. The village sits along a 3-kilometre stretch of the Timah Tasoh Flood Diversion Canal — a 22-kilometre waterway connecting Empangan Timah Tasoh all the way to the sea at Muara Kuala Perlis.

The canal's water glows in a mesmerising blue-green hue, flanked by ancient limestone karst hills over 500 million years old. Rich flora, diverse fauna, and the gentle sound of flowing water make this one of Perlis' most treasured natural destinations.

Kamarulzaman bin Ahmad — as the PIC of the kayak operation here — is your gateway to experiencing this paradise from the water itself.

About Kampung Wai Village

Kampung Wai sits approximately 3 km from Kuala Perlis town and close to the Thai border. The village is surrounded by the Nakawan limestone range and vast paddy fields, and is accessed via a small road crossing a bridge over the canal.

The canal is part of the Projek Lencongan Banjir Barat Timah Tasoh — a 22-kilometre flood diversion project that unexpectedly created one of Malaysia's most beautiful recreational waterways.

🌄

Perlis — Malaysia's Smallest State

Perlis covers just 821 km² but is rich in character — known for its vast sugar cane fields, ancient limestone caves, royal heritage, and a border culture shaped by proximity to Thailand. The state capital is Kangar, with Kuala Perlis as the main port.

Perlis is home to Gua Kelam, the Timah Tasoh Lake, Wang Kelian border market, and now Kampung Wai — all part of the Visit Perlis Year 2024–2026 eco-tourism showcase.

🏃

The Gua Kelam Connection

The kayak route is intimately connected to Gua Kelam (Cave of Darkness) in Kaki Bukit — one of Perlis' most iconic attractions. During the colonial tin-mining era, Gua Kelam's 370-metre cavern was used to transport tin ore along the underground river.

The 765-metre water tunnel at Tebing Tinggi — the highlight of the kayak experience — is a separate passage through the Nakawan range connecting Kampung Wai to Kampung Tebing Tinggi in Bintong.

THE DESTINATION

Kampung Wai — In Depth

Beyond the kayak adventure, Kampung Wai is a living village rich with engineering history, ecological wonder, cultural warmth, and natural beauty.

WHAT MAKES IT SPECIAL

Natural Highlights

From ancient limestone geology to crystalline waters, golden paddy fields, and the mystifying underground tunnel — Kampung Wai is where nature, adventure, and heritage converge.

🚯

Malaysia's Longest Water Tunnel — 765 Metres

The signature experience: paddling through the 765-metre water tunnel carved through Bukit Tebing Tinggi. Kayakers pass through in near-complete darkness and emerge into open sunlight — an experience unlike any other in the country.

💉

Emerald-Green Canal Waters

The canal is celebrated for its striking blue-green water colour caused by minerals filtered through surrounding limestone. The water is calm, clear, and so still that the limestone cliffs reflect perfectly on its surface.

🌿

Rich Flora & Fauna Along the Banks

The canal banks are lined with tropical vegetation — mangrove trees, wild ferns, hanging creepers. The area supports diverse wildlife: freshwater fish, kingfishers, monitor lizards, and numerous bird species including migratory visitors.

🈴

Golden Paddy Fields of Perlis

Surrounding Kampung Wai are Perlis' vast paddy fields — the agricultural heart of Malaysia's smallest state. The landscape shifts between vivid green planting season and golden harvest, creating one of northern Malaysia's most dramatic panoramas.

🏛

Dataran Kampung Wai — Community Hub

The lively riverside plaza is the launch point for all kayak trips and a gathering space for locals and visitors alike. Stalls sell local snacks, cold drinks, and Perlis delicacies. On weekends, dozens of kayakers prepare to take to the water.

🛴

500-Million-Year-Old Limestone Karst

The towering limestone hills flanking the canal are among the oldest geological formations in Malaysia. Part of the Nakawan Range, these dramatic karst formations create a breathtaking natural corridor along the waterway.

📷

A Photographer's Dream Destination

Kampung Wai has gone viral on social media for its extraordinary scenery. The combination of limestone backdrops, glowing emerald water, colourful kayaks, and the eerie beauty of the tunnel makes every angle a potential masterpiece.

🔲

Swimming in the Clear Canal

Beyond kayaking, visitors swim in designated areas of the canal — the water is clean and refreshing. Swimming after the Tebing Tinggi kayak trip has become a popular tradition, offering a chance to cool down and fully immerse in the natural environment.

🌮

Eco-Tourism & Visit Perlis 2024–2026

Since kayaking was introduced in 2022, Kampung Wai has transformed into one of Perlis' flagship eco-tourism sites, officially featured in the Visit Perlis Year 2024–2026 programme, attracting visitors from all over Malaysia and beyond.

KNOW THE PLACE

Behind the Scenery

Kampung Wai rewards curious visitors who look deeper — from its accidental engineering origins to its viral social media fame and community tourism model.

🌊

The Canal's Origin Story

The Kampung Wai canal was not built for tourism — it is part of the Projek Lencongan Banjir Barat Timah Tasoh, a 22-km flood diversion canal built to redirect excess water from Empangan Timah Tasoh to the sea at Kuala Perlis. This engineering project unintentionally created one of Malaysia's most scenic waterways, now beloved by paddlers nationwide.

💉

Crystal-Clear Water Quality

The canal's water is famously clean and clear. Its iconic blue-green colour is produced by the limestone mineral composition of the surrounding geology, which naturally filters and colours the water. Visibility is high enough that paddlers can often see the canal floor from their kayaks.

🈴

Paddy Field Landscapes

Perlis produces some of Malaysia's finest rice, and the paddy fields surrounding Kampung Wai are spectacular year-round. In planting season, vivid green shoots fill the paddies as far as the eye can see. At harvest time, fields turn a deep golden-yellow. From the bridge near Kampung Wai, these fields frame the limestone hills in one of northern Malaysia's most dramatic panoramas.

🍽

Local Food & Roadside Stalls

At Dataran Kampung Wai, local residents have established food and beverage stalls offering authentic Perlis village cooking — cold drinks, local snacks, simple rice dishes, and fresh coconut water. These stalls generate important supplementary income for local families and give visitors a chance to rest and enjoy riverside hospitality.

📷

Social Media Fame

Kampung Wai went viral on TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube as travellers began sharing footage of the emerald canal, dramatic limestone corridor, and the eerie beauty of the water tunnel. This organic growth has been transformational for Perlis tourism, drawing visitors who had never considered the state before.

🌮

Perlis Eco-Tourism Club

The Kampung Wai kayak operation is managed under the Perlis Eco-Tourism and Recreation Club (Kelab Eko-Pelancongan dan Rekreasi Perlis). Established to bring sustainable tourism benefits to Perlis' natural sites, the club coordinates kayaking activities, sets pricing, manages safety standards, and works to improve the visitor experience continuously.

🏆

Visit Perlis Year 2024–2026

Kampung Wai is officially featured as a key destination in the Visit Perlis Year 2024–2026 (Tahun Melawat Perlis) initiative by Tourism Perlis. This government-backed programme promotes the state's natural, cultural, and heritage attractions to domestic and international tourists.

🔲

Swimming & Riverside Recreation

Kayaking is not the only activity — swimming in the canal's clear, refreshing water is enjoyed by visitors of all ages. The Dataran also serves for riverside picnics, fish feeding from the banks, drone photography, and simply sitting by the water. Its peaceful, natural atmosphere makes it an ideal "healing" retreat from city life.

PLANNING YOUR VISIT

What to Know Before You Go

A few helpful tips to make your Kampung Wai kayak experience smooth and unforgettable.

🕑 Best Time to Visit

Weekday afternoons (Tue–Fri) are quieter. School holidays and weekends are busiest — arrive early to secure your spot. Avoid Mondays (closed).

📦 What to Bring

Wear clothes you don't mind getting wet. Bring a waterproof bag or case for your phone. Sunscreen, a hat, and water shoes are recommended.

👤 Group Bookings

School groups and large parties should coordinate in advance. During school holidays, Kamarulzaman's team can manage up to 15 trips per day for large groups.

💷 Pricing & Payment

Tebing Tinggi Package: RM 69/person. Santai Package: RM 10/person. Cash is recommended. Confirm pricing directly at Dataran Kampung Wai on arrival.

DIRECTIONS

How to Get There

Kampung Wai is accessible by car, ride-hailing app, or public transport from Kangar and Kuala Perlis.

🚘 By Car

From Kangar: approximately 20–25 minutes via Jalan Kuala Perlis. From Alor Setar: approximately 50 minutes via the North-South Expressway (R14) exit.

🚌 By Bus / Public Transport

Take a bus to Kuala Perlis, then hire a local taxi or GrabCar for the final 3 km to Kampung Wai. Ask locals for "Dataran Kampung Wai" directions.

✈️ From KL / Penang

Fly into Alor Setar (AOR) or Langkawi (LGK) then drive to Perlis. Alternatively, take the ETS train to Arau station and proceed by taxi.

📸 Photography Tips

Golden hour (late afternoon) offers the most breathtaking light on the limestone corridor. Bring a waterproof case — the emerald water is irresistibly close throughout the trip.
